# Filing Patterns — What to Extract from Each Filing Type

## SEC 10-K / 20-F (Annual Report)

### Priority Sections (extract in this order):
1. **Item 7: MD&A** (Management Discussion & Analysis)
   - Revenue discussion by segment
   - Net interest income analysis
   - Provision for credit losses discussion
   - Capital and liquidity overview
   - Forward-looking statements / outlook

2. **Item 8: Financial Statements**
   - Consolidated income statement (2+ years)
   - Consolidated balance sheet
   - Consolidated statement of cash flows
   - Note: Loans and allowance for credit losses
   - Note: Deposits and funding
   - Note: Capital ratios (regulatory capital)
   - Note: Fair value measurements
   - Note: Segment information

3. **Item 1A: Risk Factors**
   - Credit risk
   - Market risk (interest rate, FX)
   - Liquidity risk
   - Operational risk
   - Regulatory risk
   - Geopolitical risk

4. **Item 1: Business Description**
   - Business segments overview
   - Geographic footprint
   - Competitive positioning
   - Regulatory framework

### Key Data Points to Extract:
```
Income Statement: Total revenue, NII, non-interest income, provisions, opex, net income, EPS
Balance Sheet: Total assets, total loans, total deposits, securities, RWA, CET1, total equity
Ratios: CET1 ratio, leverage ratio, ROE, ROA, NIM, cost/income, NPL ratio, LCR, NSFR
```

## SEC 10-Q (Quarterly Report)

### Priority Sections:
1. **Financial Statements** (unaudited)
   - Income statement (current quarter + YTD)
   - Balance sheet (quarter-end)
   - Key notes updates
2. **MD&A** (abbreviated)
   - Quarter-over-quarter trends
   - Updated outlook
3. **Quantitative and Qualitative Disclosures About Market Risk**

### Key Delta Analysis:
- Compare to prior quarter and same quarter prior year
- Focus on: NII trend, provision trend, deposit flows, loan growth, CET1 change

## Pillar 3 Disclosure (EU Banks)

### Key Tables:
| Table | Content | Use |
|-------|---------|-----|
| EU OV1 | Overview of RWA | Total RWA, RWA by risk type |
| EU CR1 | Credit risk exposures | Gross/net exposures by asset class |
| EU CR3 | Credit risk mitigation | Collateral types and coverage |
| EU CR5 | Standardised approach | RWA breakdown |
| EU CCR1 | Counterparty credit risk | Derivatives exposure |
| EU LIQ1 | LCR | Liquidity coverage ratio detail |
| EU LR1 | Leverage ratio | Tier 1 / total exposure |
| EU MR1 | Market risk | Trading book RWA |

### Key Data Points:
```
CET1 capital, AT1 capital, Tier 2 capital, Total capital
RWA: Credit risk, Market risk, Operational risk, Total
CET1 ratio, T1 ratio, Total capital ratio
Leverage ratio (fully loaded)
LCR, NSFR
NPE ratio, NPE coverage ratio
```

## Earnings Call Transcript

### Sections to Focus On:
1. **CEO Opening Remarks** (2-3 min)
   - Revenue highlights
   - Strategic updates
   - Outlook statements

2. **CFO Financial Review** (5-10 min)
   - Detailed P&L walkthrough
   - Balance sheet changes
   - Capital deployment plans
   - Specific guidance (NII, provisions, costs)

3. **Q&A Session**
   - Analyst questions reveal market concerns
   - Management responses show confidence/hedging
   - Track specific commitments ("we expect CET1 to reach X by year-end")

### Key Quotes to Flag:
- Forward guidance on NII, provisions, costs, capital returns
- M&A commentary
- Regulatory developments
- Asset quality trends
- Technology/digital strategy updates

## Annual Report (Full Document from IR)

### What's Different from 10-K:
- Often includes: CEO letter, strategy section, sustainability report
- More visual and narrative-focused
- May include non-GAAP/non-IFRS metrics prominently
- Segment detail often more granular than 10-K

### Priority Sections:
1. CEO/Chairman letter (strategic direction, tone)
2. Financial highlights page (key metrics at a glance)
3. Risk management section (risk appetite, stress testing results)
4. Segment reviews (each business line's performance)
5. Capital management (distributions, buybacks, SREP requirements)
