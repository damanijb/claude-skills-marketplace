# Formatting Rules — SBC Treasury Credit Reports

## Banned Language

### Absolute Bans
| Banned | Replacement |
|--------|------------|
| "record growth" | "continued growth", "strong growth" |
| "record profits" | "highest since [year]", "strongest in [period]" |
| "record revenue" | "peak revenue", "above-trend revenue" |
| "a record" (noun) | Remove entirely or rephrase with specific context |
| "unprecedented" | "significant", "notable" |
| "remarkable" | "above-trend", "materially improved" |
| "exceptional" | "strong", "well above median" |
| "outstanding" | "solid", "robust" |
| "soared" | "rose", "increased" |
| "plummeted" | "declined", "fell" |
| "surged" | "expanded", "grew" |
| "collapsed" | "contracted", "compressed" |
| "skyrocketed" | "rose sharply", "increased materially" |
| "tanked" | "declined sharply", "deteriorated" |

### Preferred Verbs for Financial Movement
| Direction | Preferred Verbs |
|-----------|----------------|
| Up | rose, increased, expanded, improved, grew, advanced |
| Down | declined, fell, contracted, compressed, narrowed, decreased |
| Flat | remained stable, was unchanged, held steady |
| Mixed | was mixed, showed divergence, varied by segment |

## Number Formatting

| Value Range | Format | Example | Wrong Example |
|------------|--------|---------|---------------|
| ≥ $1B | $X.XXB | $1.30B | $1,304 million |
| $1MM–$999MM (whole) | $XMM | $809MM | $809.00MM |
| $1MM–$999MM (decimal) | $XXX.XXMM | $809.14MM | $809.1MM |
| < $1MM | $X.XK | $45.2K | $45,200 |
| Percentages (1 decimal meaningful) | X.X% | 14.2% | 14.20% |
| Percentages (whole) | X% | 3% | 3.0% |
| Basis points | XX bps | 35 bps | 0.35% (when bps intended) |
| Negative values | -$XMM | -$23MM | ($23MM) |
| Ratios | X.Xx | 1.2x | 1.20x |

### Critical Rules
- NEVER use brackets/parentheses for negative values: `-$23MM` not `($23MM)`
- NEVER combine number and change in one token: not `$800MM(+3.00%)`
- Drop `.00` on millions: `$809MM` not `$809.00MM`
- Billions always show 2 decimals: `$1.30B` not `$1.3B`

## Number-in-Context Rules

### The Pattern: Change → Number → Driver

**WRONG:**
```
Revenue was $800MM(+3.00%)
NII of $1,304 million
CET1 ratio stood at 14.2%(+20bps QoQ)
```

**RIGHT:**
```
Revenue rose 3% YoY to $800MM, driven by higher net interest income and fee growth.
NII of $1.30B reflected a 15 bps expansion in net interest margin.
CET1 ratio improved 20 bps QoQ to 14.2%, supported by organic capital generation.
```

### Structure
1. **State the change** — "rose 3% YoY", "declined 12 bps QoQ"
2. **State the value** — "to $800MM", "to 14.2%"
3. **State the driver** — "driven by...", "reflecting...", "as a result of..."

## Tone Rules

### Required Tone
- Neutral, factual, and comprehensive
- Let the data tell the story
- Balance positive and negative factors — never cherry-pick

### Attribution
| Instead of | Write |
|-----------|-------|
| "The bank achieved..." | "Management stated..." or "Results showed..." |
| "The bank excelled at..." | "The bank reported..." |
| "Impressively, revenue..." | "Revenue..." |
| "The strong performance..." | "Performance in Q4..." |

### Hedging (when appropriate)
- "Management guidance suggests..." not "The bank will..."
- "Based on current trends..." not "The bank is certain to..."
- "Consensus estimates indicate..." not "Analysts agree..."

## Section Flow

Every analytical section follows **top-line to bottom-line** order:

1. **Revenue** — Total figure, YoY/QoQ change, composition
2. **Revenue drivers** — NII vs fees vs trading, stated vs quantitative
3. **Expenses** — Total opex, cost/income ratio, efficiency programs
4. **Expense drivers** — Compensation, technology, restructuring charges
5. **Provisions** — Charge, cost of risk, NPL migration
6. **Net income** — Bottom line, EPS, ROE impact

Never jump from revenue directly to net income without covering expenses.

## Table Rules

- All tables must have a header row
- Right-align all numeric columns
- Left-align text/label columns
- Include units in headers, not in every cell: "Revenue ($B)" not "$1.30B" in header
- Use consistent decimal places within a column
- Include YoY or QoQ change columns where relevant
- Peer median row at bottom of comparison tables
- Highlight the target issuer row (bold or shading)

## Citation Rules

- Attribute all management claims: "Management noted that..." or "Per the Q4 earnings call..."
- Cite rating agency actions: "Moody's affirmed the Aa3 rating with stable outlook on [date]"
- Reference specific filings: "Per the 2024 20-F filing..."
- Quantitative claims must reference the data source or calculation basis
